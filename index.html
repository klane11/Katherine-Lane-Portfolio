About Me

After obtaining a Bachelor of Science in Psychology, I had no clue what I wanted in life or a career. I worked my way up to the title of manager in the service industry and again in the healthcare industry, but still, I yearned for a new challenge.

As time went on, I considered my options: pursuing my master's degree, finding a new job, or changing careers altogether. Eventually, after lamenting over my dilemma with a close friend, she asked, "Have you ever considered coding?" I cocked an eyebrow quizzically, "Don't you need a crazy amount of school for that?" I asked. She then proceeded to explain how diverse coding has become, how different backgrounds are constantly welcome and, most importantly, she gave me all the resources I could ask.

That was the day I became curious about this whole "coding thing". At the beginning of 2016, I barely knew the difference between front end and back end development. Thanks to heaps of meet-ups, in-person and online courses, web searches, and the never-ending patience of my IT-employed friends, I gained knowledge of programming and knew I wanted to dive in.

And so, in June of 2017, I started down a path which was notorious for its highs and lows; I resigned myself to a future of frustration-fueled tears being followed by victorious laughter...I signed up for a Coding Bootcamp.

Oh what a ride it's been. And you know what? I love it.

myPhotoAtlas

myPhotoAtlas is an application geared toward photography enthusiasts and adventurous travelers. Our product allows users to search any city or specific location around the world. Users can then set a radius and select different tags to explore, whether it's parks, piers, waterfalls, graffiti, or all of the above. Once searched, a gallery of photos within their specifications appear. Using geo coordinates, when a gallery photo is clicked the photo location is displayed on the embedded Google Map. Additional features include the ability to get directions with Google Maps and save/delete locations and images to the my ‘myPlaces’ page.
